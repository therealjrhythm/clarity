## VISUAL STYLE ARCHETYPE

**Select your primary style archetype** (choose 1-2 max, or define hybrid):

### Common Archetypes:

**1. Glassmorphism**
- Frosted glass effects, backdrop blur, transparency layers
- Soft shadows, subtle borders, depth through blur
- Modern, sophisticated, Apple-inspired aesthetic
- Best for: Tech products, modern apps, premium services

**2. Bold Minimalism**
- Extreme contrast (pure black/white or intense color + white)
- MASSIVE typography (72-120px headlines)
- Minimal elements, maximum impact
- Best for: Fintech, SaaS, confident brands, conversion-focused

**3. Dark Energetic**
- Black/dark base with glowing effects
- Particle systems, light beams, animated backgrounds
- High energy, tech-forward, gaming aesthetic
- Best for: Tech startups, gaming, social platforms, viral products

**4. 3D Product Showcase**
- Realistic 3D products with tilt, shadow, depth
- Clean backgrounds let product be hero
- Premium positioning, tangible quality
- Best for: Physical products, cards, devices, e-commerce

**5. Soft Organic (Teddy Wear example)**
- Gentle curves, muted pastels, fabric-like textures
- Warm shadows, generous spacing, breathing room
- Nostalgic, approachable, human-centered
- Best for: Lifestyle brands, handmade products, wellness

**6. Editorial Luxury**
- Generous whitespace (70%+ negative space)
- Sophisticated typography, editorial layouts
- Restrained color, high-end photography
- Best for: Fashion, luxury goods, high-end services

**7. Brutalism**
- Raw, harsh, geometric
- Exposed grid, monospaced fonts, stark contrast
- Anti-design aesthetic, intentionally challenging
- Best for: Art/culture, rebellious brands, portfolio

**8. Bento Box / Card Layouts**
- Color-blocked cards in grid system
- Each card = distinct moment/feature
- Organized, scannable, modular
- Best for: SaaS features, stats, product comparisons

**9. Maximalism**
- Dense, layered, complex visual systems
- Rich patterns, abundant decoration, no restraint
- Overwhelming in intentional way, baroque digital
- Best for: Creative agencies, entertainment, fashion-forward

**10. Neumorphism**
- Soft shadows creating embossed/extruded feeling
- Monochromatic with subtle depth
- Tactile, pressable, physical interface feel
- Best for: Apps, dashboards, music players

## HERO ANIMATION SELECTION

**Based on your motion references, select hero background approach:**

### Available Hero Background Techniques:

**Animated Natural Phenomena:**
- **Lava/Fluid Effect:** Morphing gradients, liquid motion (like Luminous reference)
  - Implementation: CSS gradients + keyframe animation OR Canvas/WebGL
  - CPU: Medium-High (3-8%)
  - Best for: Energy, power, explosive brands

- **Particle Systems:** Rising particles, floating elements, starfield
  - Implementation: Canvas 2D or WebGL for performance
  - CPU: Medium (2-5%)
  - Best for: Tech, cosmic, ethereal vibes

- **Light Beams/Shafts:** Vertical/horizontal light rays
  - Implementation: CSS linear gradients + animation
  - CPU: Low (1-2%)
  - Best for: Dramatic reveals, spotlights, divine feeling

**Geometric/Technical:**
- **Animated Grid:** Data visualization, matrix-style, technical aesthetic
  - Implementation: SVG or Canvas
  - CPU: Low-Medium (2-4%)
  - Best for: Tech, data, systematic brands

- **Gradient Mesh:** Multi-point gradients with smooth color transitions
  - Implementation: CSS or SVG gradients
  - CPU: Low (1-2%)
  - Best for: Modern, sophisticated, depth without literal objects

**3D/Spatial:**
- **WebGL Scene:** Complex 3D environments, shaders, interactive
  - Implementation: Three.js, React Three Fiber
  - CPU: High (8-15%)
  - Best for: Premium experiences, desktop-first, showcasing capability

- **Parallax Layers:** Multiple depth planes moving at different speeds
  - Implementation: CSS transforms on scroll
  - CPU: Low (1-2%)
  - Best for: Depth, polish, works on all devices

**Minimal/Clean:**
- **Subtle Texture:** Animated grain, noise, paper texture
  - Implementation: CSS background + animation
  - CPU: Very Low (<1%)
  - Best for: Sophisticated restraint, fabric feel, organic

- **Static with Vignette:** No animation, just atmospheric lighting
  - Implementation: CSS radial gradients
  - CPU: None
  - Best for: Performance-critical, minimal aesthetic, content-first

## GRID BREAKING & COMPOSITIONAL TENSION

### Grid Philosophy:

**Perfect grids work when needed, depending on project.**

**When to use strict grid:**
- Product listings (scannable)
- Blog layouts (readable)
- Data tables (organized)
- Corporate (professional)

**When to break grid:**
- Hero sections (memorable impact)
- Featured content (emphasis)
- Section transitions (signal change)
- Brand expression (personality)

### Grid-Breaking Techniques:

**Scale Contrast:**
- One element 2-3x larger (hero treatment)
- Tiny + huge pairing (tension)

**Overlapping Layers:**
- Deliberate overlap (depth)
- Content bleeds across sections

**Diagonal Flow:**
- Angled dividers (dynamic)
- Rotated elements (movement)

**Edge Bleeding:**
- Cut off at viewport (more to discover)
- Asymmetric crops (curiosity)

**Organic Containers:**
- Blobs for organic brands
- Circles for portraits
- Not everything is rectangles

**Whitespace Manipulation:**
- Generous around important (emphasis)
- Tight clusters for related (grouping)
- Asymmetric margins (tension)

### When to Break, When to Hold:

**Always maintain:**
- Body copy (readability)
- Navigation (predictability)
- Forms (functionality)

**Consider breaking:**
- Hero/featured (memorable)
- Visual emphasis (hierarchy)
- Brand expression (personality)
